# ⚡ Lightning Strikes Analysis (2016–2018)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project explores lightning strike data from 2016 to 2018 using Python and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA). The objective is to identify patterns, trends, and geographical variations in lightning activity across different locations, weekdays, and months.

By analyzing historical lightning data, this project provides insights into when and where lightning strikes occur most frequently, helping support weather-related research and risk assessment.

## 🔍 Key Findings

* Lightning activity was unevenly distributed across locations.
* Certain locations recorded exceptionally high numbers of lightning strikes in a single day.
* Some locations experienced lightning on a large number of separate days, indicating persistent storm activity.
* Lightning strike frequency varied throughout the week.
* Weekend strike counts were generally lower compared to weekdays.
* Strong seasonal patterns were observed across all three years.
* August consistently showed the highest lightning activity.
* In 2018, more than one-third of all recorded lightning strikes occurred during August.

---

## 📈 Business & Analytical Insights

* Lightning activity demonstrates significant temporal and geographical variation.
* Seasonal weather conditions play a major role in lightning occurrence.
* Identifying high-risk periods and locations can support weather forecasting and public safety planning.
* Historical lightning data can be valuable for infrastructure protection and risk management strategies.

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

This analysis highlights how lightning strikes vary across time and location. The findings reveal clear seasonal trends, recurring high-risk regions, and differences in lightning activity throughout the week. These insights contribute to a better understanding of weather behavior and can assist decision-making in forecasting, disaster preparedness, and environmental studies.
